Data Analysis Projects

Maven Analytics Pizza Sales

The Maven Analytics Pizza Sales project involved cleaning data from a pizza restaurant and querying the data to identify sales trends over the course of a year.

First, the data was cleaned to ensure accuracy during further data analysis. Several of the datasets contained duplicated entries, errors, and formatting inconsistencies.

Data was queried from multiple tables within the Maven's Pizzas database to evalute the store's sales trends over the course of one year. Measures included quantity of orders, quantity of pizzas ordered, revenue, quantity of type of pizzas, and price per order. These measures were used to determine popular days of the week, peak and lull hours, sales per quarter, and most and least popular pizzas. Knowing this information is useful to the stakeholders for making decisions about potential changes to the hours/days of operation and pizza type availability.

Finally, an interactive dashboard was created to present the data to stakeholders and help them make informed decsions about their business.

Penguin Paramedic
Senior Design Capstone

Refrigeration is an easy task to accomplish in a hospital or pharmacy setting; however, not all clinical applications take place in a stationary establishment. Administration of medications in an ambulance saves time and lives. Unfortunately, keeping these medications at the appropriate temperatures of 2-8Â°C in an ambulance poses many challenges, including temperature stability, availability of space, power consumption, heat output, and securability. Penguin Paramedic is a medical refrigeration device that maintains cool medication temperatures in an ambulance for patients receiving pre-hospital care in order to increase the stability of temperature-sensitive medications. The device achieves this outcome by utilizing thermoelectric coolers controlled by a negative feedback loop. These small coolers provide the necessary temperature range for medication storage without expelling large quantities of heat while also minimizing power consumption. Introducing the Penguin Paramedic into ambulances would expand storage capabilities in pre-hospital settings, save time in emergency rooms, and prevent medication waste.

This project included meetings with stakeholders, preparation of a proposal, documentation using a design history file, and several phases of prototyping and benchtop testing. The device was modeled using Solidworks and components were cut using a water jet. In addition to the thermoelectric coolers, fans were also used to disperse the heat generated. The circuit designed to control the thermoelectric coolers and fans included negative feedback loops with cascading switches.

The Nanny
Freshman Smart Device Design

As a parent or caregiver, bringing young children into a public area can be stressful and overhwleming. A major concern is making sure the child stays close. Whether the concern is getting separated in a busy area, the child's curiosity causing them to wander, or potential kidnapping, caregivers want peace of mind that the child will stay with them. All it takes is a few moments for a small child to disappear. Currently, the most common solutions to this problem are holding the child or using a children's harness,commonly refered to as a "child leash". Carrying a child while running errands is cumbersome and impractical, especially for those with multiple children. Some people also do not have the physical ability to carry a child. A children's harness may also be impractical for many errands, as it still requires a free hand. There is also a stigma towards parents and caregivers who use these devices because, visually, many people see it as putting a child on a dog leash and many parents do not want to experience that judgement.

The Nanny is a smart device alternative to keep the child close. It uses Arduino hardware and software to measure the distance between the parent and child and sets off an alarm when a specified distance is exceeded. Essentially, The Nanny behaves as an invisible, auditory leash, keeping the parent or caregiver hands- and judgement-free.
